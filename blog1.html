The Future of Tuna: Why Hand-line Fishing Matters?

Oceava Team

Dec 22, 2025

5 min read
"In an era where large-scale fishing industries use trawlers to sweep resources from the sea, traditional methods like 'Hand-line' have become the answer for a sustainable future."

When we talk about premium grade tuna, the first things that come to mind are taste and freshness. But behind that deliciousness, the "catching method" has a massive impact on both meat quality and the global ecosystem. Today, we'll take you through why OCEAVA stands firm on using 100% Hand-line fishing.

What is Hand-line Fishing?

Hand-line is the simplest fishing method: 1 person, 1 line, 1 hook, and 1 fish. Our fishermen take small boats out into the deep sea and use specialized skills to catch tuna one by one.

No large nets, no destructive machinery, just human patience and skill.

Did You Know?

Fish caught by hand-line experience less stress than those caught in nets, resulting in less Lactic Acid secretion in the meat. This results in beautiful red meat, no bruising, and distinctly better taste. Perfect for premium Sashimi.

Why Hand-line? (3 Key Reasons)

1

Zero Bycatch

A major problem with trawlers is catching unwanted marine life (Bycatch) like sea turtles, dolphins, or sharks. But hand-line targets only specific tuna. 100% friendly to marine friends.

2

Superior Quality

Every fish is cared for immediately upon leaving the water, entering the stunning and chilling process instantly. No piling up like in nets, resulting in beautiful, bruise-free meat.

3

Supporting Local Communities

Supporting local fisheries helps distribute income directly to the community. Over 100 families in Bitung have secure livelihoods from working with us.

Conclusion

Choosing tuna from OCEAVA isn't just buying high-quality seafood; it's being part of ocean conservation and supporting sustainable fishing livelihoods. We believe the best food is food that is cared for from the source to your plate.
